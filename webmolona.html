Office Work

03 / Well-being

My Plan for Not Burning Out

I've read way too many horror stories about people burning out while working remotely. Since I haven't even started working yet, these are strategies I'm planning to use based on what seems to work for others.

Reality check: These are my goals and plans, not things I'm currently doing. We'll see if future √Ålex actually follows through with this or if it all goes out the window once I start working. Fingers crossed!

üî• Avoiding Burnout

  • Actually log off at the end of the day. I'm planning to set hard boundaries ‚Äì work laptop closes at 6 PM and doesn't open until the next morning.
  • Use the Pomodoro technique. Everyone recommends this ‚Äì 25 minutes focused work, 5 minute break. Gonna try it and actually move around during breaks.
  • Learn to say no. This will be hard for me as a junior trying to prove myself, but I can't take every project just because I'm new.
  • Block off focus time. Planning to mark hours as "Do Not Disturb" so I can actually concentrate without interruptions.
  • Know my peak hours. I'm useless before 10 AM, so I'll schedule difficult tasks for when my brain actually works.
  • Use all vacation days. I've heard too many stories of people not taking time off. Not gonna be me.

‚ö° Staying Productive

  • Morning routine. Gonna shower and get dressed (real clothes, not pajamas) to signal to my brain that work is starting.
  • Time-blocking. Scheduling specific tasks for specific hours. Sounds rigid but should help with my procrastination problem.
  • Dedicated workspace. Not working from bed or the couch. Need one spot that's only for work.
  • Batch similar tasks. Answer all emails at once, do all meetings back-to-back. More efficient than constantly switching.
  • Use organization tools. Probably going with Notion because it looks cool. Just need to pick one and stick with it.
  • End-of-day shutdown. Review what I did, plan tomorrow, close laptop, walk away. This will be my "work is done" signal.
  • Block distracting sites. Instagram and YouTube are my kryptonite. Definitely using a website blocker during work hours.

üí™ Physical Health Goals

  • Exercise daily. Even if it's just 20 minutes. Thinking yoga or walks so I don't become a complete couch potato.
  • Consistent sleep schedule. Same bedtime every night. I'm terrible at this now but working from home could make it worse.
  • Stay hydrated. Water bottle on desk, phone reminders set. Brain needs water to function, apparently.
  • The 20-20-20 rule. Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Don't want to destroy my eyes.
  • Real meals away from the desk. Lunch is a break, not multitasking time. Actually gonna enforce this.
  • Invest in ergonomics. Saving up for a good chair and monitor stand. Future me's back will thank me.
  • Talk to actual humans. Regular video calls with friends and colleagues. Can't let my only social interaction be with my cat.
  • Go outside daily. At least once. Fresh air and sunlight are important. My gamer skin needs vitamin D.
Let's be real ‚Äì these all sound great in theory. The real test is whether I'll actually do them when I'm tired, stressed, and have deadlines. But at least I'm thinking about it now, right?
